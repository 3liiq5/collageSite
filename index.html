The university Hosts Sheikh Ahmed Salman in an Awareness-Raising Seminar

The University of Information Technology and Communications (UoITC) hosted Sheikh Ahmed Salman, the head of the Islamic Awakening Movement, in an awareness-raising seminar on the importance of the role of the media in spreading the culture of peace and tolerance. Held at the university's main campus, the seminar was attended by faculty members, students, and staff. Sheikh Ahmed Salman emphasized the significance of using the media to promote peace, tolerance, and understanding among diverse cultures and religions, while also highlighting the need for media professionals to be aware of their responsibilities and the societal impact of their work. Organized by the university's Department of Media and Communication, the seminar was part of a series of events aimed at promoting peace and tolerance in the region. UoITC President Dr. Ahmed Al-Husseini welcomed Sheikh Ahmed Salman and expressed gratitude for his participation, reiterating the university's commitment to fostering peace, tolerance, and understanding among different cultures and religions. He also noted the department's vital role in preparing students for careers in the media industry, emphasizing its dedication to promoting ethical and responsible journalism. Overall, the seminar was a successful event that united a diverse group to discuss the importance of advancing peace, tolerance, and understanding through the media, showcasing the university's commitment to these values and its aim of preparing students for successful careers in the media industry.

Biomedical Informatics College participates in the National Championship of the "Information and More"

The Biomedical Informatics College participated in the "Information and More" tournament, which was organized by the Ministry of Youth and Sports /Department of Scientific Welfare. Participation aims to enhance cultural and social awareness among students, in addition to developing their skills in communication and working within competitive teams, and through it, the College also seeks to provide a comprehensive educational environment that enhances students' capabilities on both the academic and personal levels, which contributes to preparing them to face the challenges of the future. The College was represented by student Abdullah Muhammad Youssef from the Smart Systems Department and student Tabarak Mustafa Abdul Rahman from the Bioinformatics Department, under the supervision of the Student Activities Division official, Assist. Lect. Ali Abdul Qader.
